About SSC CSHL Exam

Staff Selection Commission – Combined Higher Secondary Level SSC CHSL is a National level exam conducted by SSC every year. This exam is conducted to recruit eligible candidates for the Lower Divisional Clerk (LDC), Junior Secretariat Assistant (JSA), Postal Assistant (PA), Sorting Assistant (SA), Data Entry Operator (DEO) and DEO Grade A, posts in various ministries, departments and offices of the Government of India. Anyone who wants to work with government offices can give this SSC CHSL exam.

The Exam Pattern

Tier 1 :
  • General Intelligence (No of Questions 25)
    (Time Duration 15 Mins)
  • English Language (No of Questions 25)
    (Time Duration 15 Mins)
  • Quantitative Aptitude (No of Questions 25)
    (Time Duration 15 Mins)
  • General Awareness (No of Questions 25)
    (Time Duration 15 Mins)

The test total time is: 60 Mins


Tier 2 :
  • Essay Writing (200 – 250 words) (No of Questions 1)
    (Time Duration 30 Mins)
  • Letter/Application Writing (150 – 200 words) (No of Questions 1)
    (Time Duration 30 Mins)

The test total time is: 60 Mins


Tier 3 :

● Tier 3 will be a Skill test/Typing test for the qualifiers of Tier 1 and 2.

● Typing test will be conducted only for candidates applying for Data Entry Operator (DEO).

● Candidates have to qualify their test at the speed prescribed on their computer screen, provided by the commission at the Centre/venue notified.

● Typing test : For English the typing speed of 35 words per minute is required and for Hindi typing speed of 30 words per minute is important. Typing Test will be applicable for Postal assistant/Sorting assistant, LDC and Court Clerk post.

● Skills Test : Data entry speed of 8000 key per depression per hour on the computer. Skill test is conducted for a Data Entry Operator post. For DEO in C&AG office speed of 15000 key depression per hour is required. The duration of the test is 15 minutes.

● The final decision will be governed by the total marks obtained in Tier 1 and Tier 2.

● Only candidates who are VH/OH (affected by celebrate palsy) and OH with deformity in writing hand will be allowed 35 minutes including compensatory time 20 minutes for the skills test

The Exam Dates

SSC CHSL notification of exam is from November – December, 2020. The submission of online application of SSC CHSL exam is from December, 2020 – January, 2021. The Tier 1 of SSC CHSL exam is in March, 2021. The Tier 2 of SSC CHSL exam is on June, 2021. The date of Tier 3 of SSC CHSL exam is not yet notified by the Government of India.

THE SCORE VALIDITY

The validity of the score pursued in SSC CHSL exam is of one year. You can get your following job, which you applied for in the examination, till one year with that following score. As the time period of one year expires, then you have to attend the exam for one more time to get the job for that particular year.

Exam Eligibility

The aspirants who satisfy the eligibility criteria can apply for the examination. The age limit of the applicants should be from 18 – 27 years to apply for SSC CHSL examination. The candidates should have passed XII (10+2) from a specific board who are applying for the post of LDC/JSA, PA/SA, DEO.

Exam Fee

● Cancellation Fee: There are no charges for the cancellation of SSC CHSL exams.

● GATE Rescheduling Fee: The application fees of SSC CHSL exam is Rs. 100/- . However, women, SC, ST, PwD and Ex-servicemen are exempted from the fees payment. Only eligible and successfully registered candidates will get admit cards in February, 2021.

How to Apply

Official website : https://www.ssc.nic.in/

Step 1: Open the official website of SSC CHSL examination ssc.nic.in

Step 2: Click on ‘Register now’ option displayed on the screen.

Step 3: Fill your personal details in the form which are asked like Name, Date of Birth, Mobile number, Email ID, Educational Background, etc.

Step 4: Upload your passport size photo and signature in the given columns.

Step 5: Click on ‘Register’ button below the form.

Step 6: Pay SSC CHSL application fee.

Benefits of taking the EXAM

The candidates who cleared the SSC CHSL examination will receive a good job and number of allowances along with the SSC CHSL post salary. It makes the job profile more attractive. The allowance they will receive are such as Dearness allowance, House Rent allowance, Travel allowance, etc. Being group ‘C' post. The jobs under SSC CHSL are clerical level jobs. Two main jobs under SSC CHSL are LDC (Lower Divisional Clerk) and DEO (Data Entry Operator). Both the jobs offer comfortable working conditions and if you stick to the job you can also be promoted to the higher post too.

Career Scope after Exam

Lower Divisional Clerk (LDC)/ Junior Secretariat Assistant (JSA) : Lower Divisional Clerk (LDC) and Junior Secretariat Assistant (JSA) is first level Clerk in any government organization. They are associated with daily office tasks and required to take responsibility to maintain the files, data and documents of the office in a systematic manner.

Postal Assistant (PA)/ Sorting Assistant (SA) : Postal Assistant (PA) and Sorting Assistant (SA) has the duty of Disposing Mails and Maintaining data. They have to support customers, handle queries and deliver a feasible solution. They have to manage all the tasks which are related to their work. They have to locate the correct address of the addressee.

Data Entry Operator (DEO) : Data Entry Operator (DEO) responsibilities include collecting and entering data in databases and maintaining accurate records of valuable company information. Our ideal candidate has essential data entry skills, like fast typing with an eye for detail and familiarity with spreadsheets and online forms.

● Data Entry Operator (DEO)
