What is RRB Group D 2020?

RRB Group D 2020 exam dates for Computer-Based Test (CBT) will be declared in August. The Exam is conducted to select eligible candidates for Level-I posts such as Track Maintainer Grade-IV, Helper/Assistant Pointsman. RRC Level 1 exam is conducted by Railway Recruitment Boards on behalf of Railway Recruitment Cells (RRCs). Selected candidates will be posted in 16 railway zones. Candidates qualifying the CBT will have to undergo PET, document verification and medical examination.

The Exam Pattern

RRB Group D exam pattern 2020, the CBT is been divided into four sections, General Science, Mathematics, General Intelligence and Reasoning, and General Awareness and Current Affairs. There will be 100 questions in the question paper. RRC Group D syllabus for CBT is given below for different sections.

About CBT:

All the eligible candidates have to undergo a Computer Based Test(s) on the specified date(s), time and venue(s) as per the e-call letter to be downloaded by the candidates from the websites of RRBs/RRCs. Candidate will be provided with e-Call Letter which will be essential while appearing.

Section-wise distribution of questions:
  • General Science (No of Questions 25)
    (Max. Marks 25)
  • Mathematics (No of Questions 25)
    (Max. Marks 25)
  • General Intelligence and Reasoning (No of Questions 30)
    (Max. Marks 30)
  • General Awareness and Current Affairs (No of Questions 20)
    (Max. Marks 20)

Total test marks is 100 and The test total time is: 120 Mins


Evaluation Scheme: :

For each correct answer in CBT, candidates will score 1 mark. There is always negative marking for wrong answers.


The Exam Dates

RRB Group D 2020 exam dates will come soon on the official website. The finalization of the Exam Conducting body is under process. Once it is finalized, RRB Group D dates will be announced on the official website. Date of exams can be delayed due to the Covid-19 pandemic. RRB Group D dates 2020 will have important events related to the exam such as the release of the application form, issue of admit card, declaration of result, etc. Candidates need to be active regarding RRB Group D dates so that they do not miss out any events related to the exam.

Exam Eligibility

The eligibility criteria of RRB Group D as per nationality, educational qualification, and age limit are given below. To appear in RRB Group D exam candidates need to fulfil the below specified criteria of age limit, nationality and education qualification. Only those who fulfil these all criteria will be selected for further examination process. At the time of document verification, the examination authority will verify the certificates given in support of the eligibility. If any anomalies in terms of eligibility criteria, candidates will be rejected from the examination process.

Nationality:

A candidate must be either:

(A) A citizen of India, or

(B) A subject of Nepal or Bhutan, or

(C) A Tibetan refugee who came to India, before January 1, 1962, with the intention of permanently settling in India, or

(D) A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, East African countries of Kenya, Uganda, the

United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia and Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India.

Provided that the Government of India had issued eligibility certificate to candidates belonging to (b), (c), and (d)

Age Limit:

Candidates should be aged between 18 and 33 years.

Relaxation in the upper age limit is given to candidates belonging to reserved categories. The details have been shared below.

Category Age Relaxation (In years)
OBC 3
SC/ST 5
PWD 10
PWD SC/ST 15

Educational Qualification:

The Candidates who had completed Matriculation (10th ) from Institution recognized by NCVT/SCVT or equivalent or National Apprenticeship Certificate (NAC) granted by NCVT were eligible to apply. ITI was made non-mandatory for Group D recruitment.

* Note:

● Those awaiting results of the final examination of the prescribed minimum educational qualification SHOULD NOT apply.

● Diploma/Degree in Engineering will not be accepted in lieu of the course completed Act Apprenticeship / ITI. Also, Graduate Act Apprentice will not be accepted in lieu of Course Completed Act Apprenticeship.

Step by Step Procedure to Apply on Website:

Step 1: Candidates must visit the official website http://www.indianrailways.gov.in/ and click on the “Railway Group D Recruitment Notification” link.

Step 2: Read the instructions carefully and click on the “Apply Online button”.

Step 3: Select “New Registration” if applying for the first time and not registered before.

Step 4: Fill up all the details asked such as personal, contact details and academic details.

Step 5: Upload scanned photograph and signature in the prescribed format and size.

Step 6: Make the payment of the RRB Group D application fee (details are shared above).

Step 7: Review the information provided and Submit the RRB Group D online application form.

Step 8: Take the print out of RRB Group D application form for future reference.
