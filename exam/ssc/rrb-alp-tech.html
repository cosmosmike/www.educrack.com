A Brief Study About RRB ALP/Tech EXAM

Railway Recruitment Board is conducting RRB ALP and Technician Exam to recruit Assistant Loco Pilot and Technician. RRB ALP exam is being conducted in all states of India Individually by the Different Railway Zones. After getting eligible with age and qualification criteria candidates will appear in the 3 stage examination process. The examination is being conducted online where candidates must have basic computer knowledge in order to attempt the exam. Recruitment will consider marks scored and rank acquired by candidates At last it will be the document verification process. After all these stages RRB will declare results on their respective zonal websites.

The Exam Pattern

The entire recruitment process shall involve, First stage CBT, Second stage CBT, Computer Based Aptitude Test and Document Verification as applicable. First two stages are common for both the ALP and Technician. First Stage CBT, which will eliminate candidates based on the score in the exam. Score of the First Stage CBT exam merit will become a pass to get in Second Stage CBT. Third Stage is CBAT, An aptitude test only for candidates who have applied for Assistant Loco Pilot. And at the last will be the document verification round.

Recruitment process includes two CBT exams.

First CBT and Second Stage CBT exams are common for ALP and technicians. The second stage has two parts Part A and Part B.

First Stage CBT :
  • Mathematics (No of Questions 25)
  • General Intelligence & Reasoning (No of Questions 25)
  • 3. Basic Science and Engineering (No of Questions 40)
  • General Awareness and Current Affairs (No of Questions 10)

The test total time is: 60 Mins


Second Stage CBT :

Part A

  • Mathematics (No of Questions 25)
  • General Intelligence & Reasoning (No of Questions 25)
  • 3. Basic Science and Engineering (No of Questions 40)
  • General Awareness and Current Affairs (No of Questions 10)

The test total time is: 60 Mins


Part B

  • Relevant Trade (No of Questions 75)

The test total time is: 60 Mins


*There is negative marking for incorrect answers, ⅓ mark will be deducted for every wrong answer in these CBTs.

How to Apply?

Sole responsibility lays with the candidates to prove with valid documents that all the information submitted by them in the ONLINE application is true.

Important Clarification Regarding Communication: Candidates are advised to keep their personal mobile number and a valid email active throughout the recruitment process, as all communications from RRBs will be only through SMS/email. The RRB does not entertain any request for change of mobile number and email address at any stage. Candidates are advised to note and preserve their Registration Number for further stages of the recruitment process / correspondence with RRBs.

Step by Step Procedure to Apply on Website:

Candidates must visit the respective RRB websites to find the online application link for RRB ALP and technician. Click on the “Apply” link.

Then one can follow the below steps to complete the application -

Step 1 - Complete registration process by entering your name, date of birth, father’s name, mother’s name, aadhaar number, state, community, SSLC/Matric roll number, year of passing SSLC/Matric, mobile number and email-id.

Step 2- Submit the details entered. Post completion of RRB ALP registration, OTPs (One Time Password) shall be sent to the registered mobile number and email along with the registration details.

Step 3- Next, the candidates need to provide details of Educational qualification, Community, Gender, Religion, Caste/Category etc. Save the details.

Step 4- Then, the candidates are directed to the payments section. The application fee can be paid online and offline, both. For online payment, candidates can choose net banking/credit card/debit card/UPI. In case of an offline payment, either a Bank challan or a Post Office Challan can be availed.

Step 5- After successful payment update, candidates are automatically redirected to Part II of application for more details such as the educational qualifications, choice of exam language for first and second stage CBT and more.

Step 6- Upload the recent scanned photograph as per the specifications mentioned in the official notification. It is advisable to keep this document ready before beginning to apply.

Step 7 - Candidates belonging to SC/ST have to upload a scanned copy of their category certificates (JPG/JPEG format, 50kb – 100kb), for availing the facility of free travel authority (Second Class Railway Pass).

Step 8 – Click on the declaration box to assert that all details provided are correct. Proceed to final submission. Submit the application form.

Step 9 - Take a printout of the application form for future reference.
