ABOUT CIVIL SERVICES

The Civil Services refer to those career bureaucrats of the Republic of India who are known to form the backbone of the administrative mechanism of the country. To get selected for these services, the Union Public Service Commission conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE), simply referred to as UPSC exam, a nationwide competitive examination in India. This examination is considered to be one of the most difficult examinations in India. If an aspirant successfully passes the exam, one can expect a placement in various Civil Services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and Indian Police Service (IPS).


IMPORTANCE

Civil Services is one of the most esteemed jobs in India. UPSC Civil Services or IAS Exam is conducted for recruitment to over 24 posts in the central government and its various departments. Since after independence, Indian Government workers have enjoyed high-status, prestige and power and are immensely respected for the job they do. In addition to this, Civil Service Employees are highly paid along with additional incentives, bonuses and perks, provided lavish bungalows with caretakers by the Government in order to lead a luxurious lifestyle. The youth in India have now deemed Civil Services as a “Dream” career.

About UPSC – IAS Exam

Indian Administrative Service (IAS) was formerly known as the Imperial Civil Service (ICS) is the Civil Services Examination and one of the toughest competitive exams in India. It is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission for the recruitment of officers for the All India Administrative Civil Service.

Candidates who clear all the three stages of the IAS exam enter into the prestigious civil services of the country, and become officers in the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and a host of other services. Indian Administrative Service (IAS) has been termed as one of the prestigious services among the 24 government services like IPS, IFS (Foreign), IFS (Forest), IP & TAFS, IAAS, IRS, IDAS, CMSE, IRTS, IRAS, IRPS, CAPF-AF to name a few. The Above Mentioned Examinations are conducted by UPSC in the form of Civil Services Examination (CSE) for selecting the appropriate candidates for the jobs.

The Exam Pattern

Stage I: Preliminary Examination (IAS Prelims)

Stage II: Mains Examination (IAS Mains)

Stage III: UPSC Personality Test (IAS Interview)

UPSC examination is conducted in three phases - a preliminary examination consisting of two objective-type papers (General Studies Paper I and General Studies Paper-II also popularly known as Civil Service Aptitude Test or CSAT), and a main examination consisting of nine papers of conventional (essay) type, in which two papers are qualifying and only marks of seven are counted followed by a personality test (interview).

The candidates can select their medium of writing the UPSC Civil Services IAS Mains Exam as Hindi or English or any other language listed in the 8th Schedule of the Indian Constitution. The UPSC personality test will be held only in the UPSC Bhavan in New Delhi.

Exam Eligibility

● Candidate must be a citizen of India

● Candidates must hold a Bachelor's Degree from any of the recognized universities and those who are awaiting results or those who are yet to appear for the qualifying examination are also eligible for the Preliminary Examination.

● A candidate should be a minimum of 21 years and a maximum of 32 years old as of August 01, 2020.

Exam Fee

Candidates can make the payment through online or offline mode For online payment, candidates may use a credit card/debit card/Rupay card or Internet Banking Services.

For offline payment, download the e-challan and submit the fee in cash at SBI bank branches on the next day itself. The detailed Application Form Fee for examination is INR 100.

After fee payment, download and save the filled-in IAS 2021 Application Form. Female applicants and SC/ST/ PWD category candidates are exempted from paying the fee.

How to Apply

Step 1: Visit the official site of UPSC – www.upsc.gov.in

Step 2: Go to the ''Apply Online'' tab. Click on ''Online Application for Various Examinations'' Find the Civil Services (Preliminary) Examination Start IAS registration with Part-I.

Step 3: Fill-up the personal details, educational qualification, and address.

Step 4: Pay the prescribed application fee (Rs 100) Choose the exam center Upload the photo, sign, and photo identity card.

Step 5: Accept the declaration. Check the complete details and submit and take a printout of the IAS application form for future reference.

Benefits of taking the EXAM

Civil Services Examination (CSE) is one of the most popular exams conducted by UPSC. The Indian Administrative Services brings you into the direct channel of the government decision-making process. You get good perks and salaries which will be enough to make the first and second generation prosperous and you can contribute to society and become a role model by taking honest decisions and actions.

Career Scope after Exam

IAS Preparation is not a process, it is a way of life. IAS can be employed by Union government, state governments, and/or public-sector undertakings and gets exposure in numerous job roles like the collector, head of public sector units, commissioner, chief secretary, cabinet secretary, etc.

There is a common myth that only prestigious posts like IAS, IPS, etc come under UPSC. But the fact is various posts including both group A and group B come under this commission. Namely,

(i) Indian Administrative Service.

(ii) Indian Foreign Service.

(iii) Indian Police Service.

(iv) Indian P & T Accounts & Finance Service, Group ‘A’.

(v) Indian Audit and Accounts Service, Group ‘A’.

(vi) Indian Revenue Service (Customs and Central Excise).
