ABOUT CIVIL SERVICES

The Civil Services refer to those career bureaucrats of the Republic of India who are known to form the backbone of the administrative mechanism of the country. To get selected for these services, the Union Public Service Commission conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE), simply referred to as UPSC exam, a nationwide competitive examination in India. This examination is considered to be one of the most difficult examinations in India. If an aspirant successfully passes the exam, one can expect a placement in various Civil Services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and Indian Police Service (IPS).


IMPORTANCE

Civil Services is one of the most esteemed jobs in India. UPSC Civil Services or IAS Exam is conducted for recruitment to over 24 posts in the central government and its various departments. Since after independence, Indian Government workers have enjoyed high-status, prestige and power and are immensely respected for the job they do. In addition to this, Civil Service Employees are highly paid along with additional incentives, bonuses and perks, provided lavish bungalows with caretakers by the Government in order to lead a luxurious lifestyle. The youth in India have now deemed Civil Services as a “Dream” career.

About UPSC – IFS Exam

The Indian Forest Service (IFS) exam is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission for the recruitment officers at the All India level by the Government of India for the scientific management of its forests.

IFS is one of the 3 most known All India Services. This exam is conducted for positions in the forestry services of India. The other two services being Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and Indian Police Service (IPS).

IFS services are placed under various state and joint cadres though they have a mandate to serve both, the State and Central Governments.

The main directive of the IFS service is the implementation of the National Forest Policy which aims to ensure environmental stability and protective maintenance of the ecological balance.

An IFS officer is largely independent of district administration and exercises administrative, judicial and financial powers in one’s own domain. It is one of the top-most positions in Civil Services.

Exam Eligibility

Nationality:

A candidate must be

● A citizen of India, or

● The subject of Nepal or Bhutan, or

● Tibetan refugee who came over to India before 1st January 1962 with the intention of permanently settling in India, or

● A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, East African countries like Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia, and Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India.

NOTE - Candidates belonging to categories b, c, and d should produce a certificate of eligibility, issued by the Government of India.

Educational Qualification:

● Aspirants must hold a Bachelor’s degree with knowledge of at least one of the below-mentioned subjects namely – Animal Husbandry & Veterinary Science, Botany, Chemistry, Geology, Mathematics, Physics, Statistics and Zoology or a Bachelor’s degree in Agriculture or Forestry or Engineering of a recognised university or equivalent.

● Aspirants appearing at their degree examination (with prescribed subjects) can also compete. If they qualify the written part of the examination, they must submit the proof of passing the required examination along with their detailed application form at that stage.

Age Limit:

Age of a candidate must be between 21 years and 30 years on 1st July of the year of examination.

While the upper age limit will be relaxed as per government guidelines:

● Scheduled Caste/ Scheduled Tribe (SC /ST) Category: Up to 5 years

● Other Backward Classes (OBC) Category: Up to 3 years

● State of Jammu & Kashmir (Domiciled during the period from the 1st January 1980 to 31st December 1989: Up to 5 years.

● Defence Services personnel: Up to 5 years

● Ex-servicemen (Commissioned officers and ECOs/SSCOs) who have rendered at least 5 years Military Service as on and have been released: Up to 5 years

● Blind, deaf-mute and Orthopaedically handicapped (specially abled people): Up to 10 years.

Exam Centers in India

● Generally held at the locations like Delhi, Haryana, Punjab, Rajasthan, Gujarat etc.

● Applicants should note that there will be an upper limit on the number of candidates allotted to each Centre, except Chennai, Dispur, Kolkata and Nagpur.

● Allotment of Centres is done on "first-apply-first allot" basis. Once the capacity of a particular Centre is attained, it’s frozen.

● The Main Examination is held at centres located in Bhopal, Chennai, Delhi, Dispur (Guwahati), Hyderabad, Kolkata, Lucknow, Nagpur, Port Blair and Shimla.

Number of attempts:

The maximum number of attempts restricted for aspirants are as follows:

● General Category: 7 attempts.

● Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe Category: No restriction.

● Other Backward Classes (OBC) Category: 9 attempts.

● Physically handicapped (Specially Abled) - 9 attempts for general and OBC, while unlimited for SC/ST.

Appearing for 1 or more papers of examination is calculated as 1 attempt made at the examination even if the candidate failed to qualify for the selection.

Roles and Responsibilities

IFS officers correspond to the diplomacy, trade and cultural relations of the country. Their responsibilities include -

● Representing India in its embassies, high commissions, consulates and multilateral organizations.

● Protecting India’s national interests in the country wherever an officer is stationed.

● Improving and promoting cordial relations between India and the country that the officer is stationed in.

● Informing about the developments in the foreign country that could affect India's policies and laws.

The role of an IFS officer is truly a challenging one. Some of them are:

● An IFS officer is appointed to represent India on international forums and platforms. An IFS officer thus plays a vital role in shaping Indian foreign policies and building all diplomatic and economic ties with countries.

● To manage all diplomatic relations between India and the country they are appointed in for.

● To create, co-create and maintain economic cooperation between India and other countries.

● To persistently keep updating Indian MEA office, about recent buildouts.

Primary Benefits

Indian diplomats form the fundamental base of Indian foreign policy and channelize the correlation between India and the world. An IFS officer has the freedom of communication for the discharge of his functions.

IFS Officers enjoy VVIP status wherein they are not questioned while leaving the country even if they are under heavy debt.

IFS Officers are immune from payment of taxes. No one can file a suit to recover that amount.

IFS Officers are given the unique opportunity to represent the 1.34 billion Indians in Foreign countries.
