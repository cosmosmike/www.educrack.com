ABOUT CIVIL SERVICES

The Civil Services refer to those career bureaucrats of the Republic of India who are known to form the backbone of the administrative mechanism of the country. To get selected for these services, the Union Public Service Commission conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE), simply referred to as UPSC exam, a nationwide competitive examination in India. This examination is considered to be one of the most difficult examinations in India. If an aspirant successfully passes the exam, one can expect a placement in various Civil Services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and Indian Police Service (IPS).


IMPORTANCE

Civil Services is one of the most esteemed jobs in India. UPSC Civil Services or IAS Exam is conducted for recruitment to over 24 posts in the central government and its various departments. Since after independence, Indian Government workers have enjoyed high-status, prestige and power and are immensely respected for the job they do. In addition to this, Civil Service Employees are highly paid along with additional incentives, bonuses and perks, provided lavish bungalows with caretakers by the Government in order to lead a luxurious lifestyle. The youth in India have now deemed Civil Services as a “Dream” career.

About UPSC – CSAT Exam

IAS is the most sought-after exam in India conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC). Union Public Service Commission,i.e., UPSC is responsible for conducting IAS exam (officially known as Civil Services Exam). Those who clear a total of three stages of this exam enters into prestigious and reputable civil Services of the country, and attain positions like Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS). It is considered as one of the toughest and tricky paper but with the right approach candidates can clear this exam in the very first attempt.This exam has total three stages. The first stage is Preliminary stage. The second paper in prelims is CSAT ,stands for Civil Services Aptitude Test. It was introduced in 2011 to test analytical skills, reasoning ability and aptitude of IAS aspirant.It is officially known as General studies paper II.

The Exam Pattern

The GS Paper II (CSAT) in IAS exam is of qualifying nature and candidates should have a score of 33 per cent in this paper to qualify to the next stage of the IAS exam i.e. the Mains. The paper is of 200 marks,and Number of questions are 80.

• Exam Frequency - Once in a year

• Exam Mode - Offline - Pen & Paper Mode

• Exam Stage - Prelims - Mains (written) - Mains (interview)

• Application Fee for General/EWS/OBC – Rs. 100 for Prelims and Rs. 200 for mains

• Language - Prelims - English & Hindi

Take a look at the syllabus of CSAT exam:

1. Comprehension

2. Interpersonal skills including communication skills

3. Logical reasoning and analytical ability

4. Decision making and problem solving

5. General mental ability

6. Basic numeracy (numbers and their relations, orders of magnitude etc.) (Class X level), Data Interpretation (charts, graphs, tables, data sufficiency etc. - Class X level)

7. English Language Comprehension skills (Class X level). Questions will not be translated to Hindi.

Reference link:https://www.upsc.gov.in/examinations/revised-syllabus-scheme

Marking scheme: Candidates are awarded 2.5 marks for correct answer, and for each incorrect response 0.83 marks will be deducted.

Exam Eligibility

1. The minimum age for appearing in the exam is 21 years and maximum age is 32 years of the academic year as on August 1st.

2. For IAS And IPS services, the applicant must be Indian.

3. Aspirants must have a bachelors degree. Candidates who are waiting for their final results, can also give the exam.

4. For general category, 6 attempts are allowed.

NOTE - A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, East African countries of Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia and Vietnam with the intention of permanently setting in India can also apply for this exam.

How to Apply

Step 1: Aspirants need to go to the official website of UPSC.

Step 2: Then, look for the "Active Examinations" tab and the post for CSAT exam application notification.

Step 3: Then, register for the exam first giving the Name, Father's name, Mother's name, Contact, Email. This will be Part-A registration form. All further communication will be done via these details.

Step 4: They will have to fill the OTP for one-time registration and their Part-A Registration will be over.

Step 5: For Part-B Registration, the candidates need to fill the form with their educational details, exam centre preferences, documents image uploads.

Step 6: Then pay the application fee to complete the application form. This is the final step to apply for the UPSC CSAT Exam.

IAS Preliminary exam is conducted in about 72 cities across India. Make sure to visit the official UPSC website to shortlist the preferred center.

CSAT Exam Day Guidelines

• Candidates must Carry their admit card.

• Candidates must have their one photo-identity

• Carry black ball pen for marking response.

• Misconduct during exam is not tolerated and serious actions will be taken.

• Strict action is taken against those candidates found cheating.

• Get good sleep before heading for the exam.

Career Prospects after UPSC-CSAT exam

UPSC recruits the candidates for Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS) and Central Civil Services including the Indian Foreign Services through this exam. All the ministers and the departments in the union and state Government are headed by the IAS officers. The topper of the IAS exam has the opportunity to become the Cabinet Secretary to the Government of India which is the highest bureaucratic position one person can ever hold in India The basic per month salary of an IAS officer starts at Rs. 56,100 (TA, DA and HRA are extra). Positions like ASP/SDM/ Asst. Commissioner, ADM/Deputy Secretary/ Undersecretary, DM/ Joint Secretary/ Deputy Secretary, DM/ Special Secretary cum director/ Director, Divisional Commissioner/ Secretary Cum Commissioner/ Joint Secretary, Divisional Commissioner/ Principal Secretary/ Additional Secretary, Additional Chief Secretary, Chief Secretary, and Cabinet Secretary of India.
