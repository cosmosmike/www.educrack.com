A Brief Study About CLAT Exam

Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is a centralized national level exam that is conducted for the UG and PG law courses offered at 22 NLU and most private colleges and universities that accept the exam scores. Law is a 5-Year Integrated Programme offering degrees such as BA LLB, BBA LLB, BSc. LLB, B.Com. LLB with specialization in Civil Law, Tax Laws, Patent Laws, Criminal Laws, International Laws, Labour Laws, Cyber law, and many more such specializations, unlike the traditional form of studying law.

Prior to 2018, the exam was conducted by NLU on a rotational basis but from 2019, the exam is now conducted by the permanent body formed by the universities i.e the Consortium of NLU. The consortium conducts the CLAT mock test for UG and PG courses. This year the exam will be conducted in online mode at various centers across India. The exam is conducted annually in English language.

The Exam Pattern of CLAT

  • English including comprehension (No of Questions 28 - 32)
  • Logical reasoning (No of Questions 35 - 39)
  • Legal reasoning (No of Questions 28 - 32)
  • Current affairs including general knowledge (No of Questions 35 -39)
  • Quantitative techniques (No of Questions 13 -17)

The test total time is: 2 hours


For every right answer, the candidate is awarded one mark. There is also a negative marking scheme for every wrong answer of 0.25. There are no deductions for the questions that are not attempted by the candidate.

The Exam Dates

The CLAT 2020 exam will be conducted on August 22 in the socially distanced computer based testing mode.

The Score Validity of CLAT Paper

The CLAT score is applicable for only one year.

CLAT Exam Eligibility

Students who have completed their 10+2 are eligible to apply for the exam.

The eligibility criteria are as follows:

● 45% marks at least in class 10+2 for general/ OBC/specially-abled children.

● 40% marks in class 10+2 scheduled class or the scheduled tribe categories.

● Students who are appearing in their class 12 examinations in March/April 2020 are eligible for the CLAT exam 2020. But the students have to give the evidence of passing the class at the admission time.

● There is no specified age limit for the exam.

Exam Fee

The application fee is as given:

● For general, OBC- Rs 4000

● ST/SC- Rs 3500

Withdrawal charges - The candidates can withdraw the application for CLAT by paying a fee of

1. INR 400 for the SC/ST candidates and

2. INR 500 for the general and OBC category.

How to Apply for the CLAT Exam?

To apply for the exam, candidates can visit the official website - www.consortiumofnlus.ac.in.

Step 1: Start with the online registration on the website for the CLAT exam

Step 2: Fill the online application form as per the instruction

Step 3: Upload the documents in the prescribed format

Step 4: Pay the fee as per the applicable methods

Here is the list of documents that are required for applying the CLAT 2020:

● Photographs

● Signature

● Category certificate for SC,ST, OBC, P.W.D, and BPL candidates.

The admission process to UG and PG programs in participating universities under CLAT-2020 is completed through centralized online counseling conducted by the CLAT Consortium Office. The admission process for CLAT 2020 starts with filling of the application form and then qualifying in the national-level exam.

CLAT Admit Card

After the application process is complete, and the aspirant has registered successfully, the admit card is made available for download from the official website of CLAT consortium. The card contains the following information about the aspirant -

  • ★ Candidate's name
  • ★ Date of birth.
  • ★ Roll number
  • ★ Father's name
  • ★ Test date
  • ★ Test time
  • ★ Test City
  • ★ Examination reporting time
  • ★ Application number
  • ★ Category applied for
  • ★ Course applied for
  • ★ Photograph
  • ★ Specimen signature
  • ★ Invigilator signature
  • ★ Candidate's signature

Benefits of taking the EXAM

With a good CLAT rank, you will end up with the top law universities in India. This will assure you with a good career in the law firm. With the help of the CLAT score, you can get the best law schools after the NLU. Also, you can opt for the 5 years’ programs after class 12 directly. Apart from the NLU, even many private law colleges accept CLAT marks for the admissions.

The salary packages offered by various organisations after completing LAW courses --

1. Law firms - 70,000 to 1,80,000

2. Corporate Law - 55,000 to 1,20,000

3. Litigation - Up to 80,000

4. NGOs - 40,000 to 70,000
