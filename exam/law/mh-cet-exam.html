A Brief Study About MH CET Exam

MH – CET LAW exam is conducted by Directorate of Higher Education, Maharashtra. It is also known as Maharashtra Law CET. It is a state level entrance exam conducted for the candidates for admission in 3 and 5 year Law courses. The candidate with the highest number of marks will be offered admission in Law university. This exam is conducted to check the capacity of the candidates.

THE SCORE VALIDITY

The score validity of the MH – CET LAW exam is one year. Candidates can apply in any university in Maharashtra with the marks he scored. Admission in colleges are based on the marks scored by the candidates in MH – CET LAW EXAM. Once the duration of one year expires, then the Candi have to reappear for the examination for admission.

Exam Eligibility

A candidate who has completed an undergraduate degree and PG degree of 2/3 years duration along with the basic qualification in format of 10+2+3 are eligible to apply for MH – CET LAW EXAMS. Candidates in their final year of graduation are also eligible to apply for MH – CET LAW exam. Percentage required to apply for MH-CET Law exam is :

General : 45%

VJNT/SBC/OBC : 42%

SC/ST : 40%

Exam Fee

The fees for MH – CET LAW exam is divided into two groups

General – Rs. 800

SC/ST/OBC – Rs. 400

Application fees will not be refunded to the candidates after cancellation.

● Cancellation Fee: Cancellation charges are zero

How to Apply for the Exam?

To apply for the exam, candidates can visit the official website - mahacet.org

Step 1: Open the official website of Directorate of Higher Education (DHE), Maharashtra and click on the ‘New Registration’ tab.

Step 2: Fill the application form

Step 3: Upload photo and signature

Step 4: Pay the application fee

Step 5: Submit the application form

Benefits of taking the EXAM

The MH – CET LAW exam is conducted to get fair admission in the colleges. The institutes give admission to the students according to their marks in MH-CET Law examination.
