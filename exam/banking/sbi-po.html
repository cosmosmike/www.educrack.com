ABOUT BANK EXAMS

Securing a Bank Job and getting placed in the Banking sector has been one of the most sought after career streams. To get into RBI, SBI & its associate banks, Nationalised Banks, Private sector banks, few International banks, and a large number of small to medium cooperative banks along with Gramin Banks, aspirants need to prepare for the banking exams conducted by the Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS), Reserve Bank of India (RBI), State Bank of India (SBI), and private banks. Exams like SBI PO, SBI Clerk, IBPS PO, IBPS Clerk, IBPS RRB, IBPS SO, RBI Grade B, RBI Assistant, etc. are conducted for recruitment in the banking sector.


IMPORTANCE

About SBI PO Exam

SBI PO exam is taken each year to select eligible candidates for the post of Probationary Officer. State Bank of India conducts these exams and a large number of candidates apply for it. Overall, SBI PO recruitment involves preliminary exam, mains exam, group discussion and interview. The contenders have to clear all of the three stages for the SBI PO post. Here, the final selection is merit-based but only a few students are selected due to the complexity of the exam.

The overall examination is considered tough because data Interpretation and reasoning sections are very difficult. While the English and General Awareness sections are often easy to moderate in the exam. The candidates that pass out are posted as Probationary Officers in the various branches of the bank. Many times, they are posted anywhere in the country depending on the number of vacancies.

SBI PO is regarded as the most sought after banking exams in the country.

The Exam Pattern

Stage 1 : Preliminary Examination
  • English Language (No of Questions 30)
    (Duration in Mins 20)
  • Quantitative (No of Questions 35)
    (Duration in Mins 20)
  • Reasoning Ability (No of Questions 35)
    (Duration in Mins 20)

The test total time is: 60 Mins


Stage 2 : Mains Exam
  • Reasoning & Computer Aptitude (No of Questions 45)
    (Duration in Mins 60)
  • Data Analysis & Interpretation (No of Questions 35)
    (Duration in Mins 45)
  • General/ Economy/ Banking Awareness (No of Questions 40)
    (Duration in Mins 35)
  • English Language (No of Questions 35)
    (Duration in Mins 40)

The test total time is: 3 hours

The Exam Dates

The State Bank of India conducts the exam for the post of PO and its Associate banks. Last year, the preliminary exam was held in mid of June and the mains were held in the last week of July. This year, the SBI PO 2020 dates are postponed due to the massive lockdown. Hence, the candidates appearing for the SBI PO 2020 exam will have to wait for the official release date. The recruitment process will start once the official notification is announced.

Exam Score

Exam Marks weightage
Mains 75
Group Exercises & Interview 25
Total 100

THE SCORE VALIDITY

The preliminary exam has 100 questions each carrying 1 mark and a wrong answer carrying a penalty of -0•25. The preliminary exam is the first stage of the recruitment but its marks will not be added for the final selection of candidates. Eventually, the final SBI PO merit list is prepared on the basis of marks obtained in the Mains exam as well as Group Exercises and Interview. Candidates qualifying the preliminary exam will appear for the mains MCQ based exam. The authorities decide category-wise SBI PO cut off marks to select candidates for the post.

Exam Eligibility

For the SBI PO Exam 2020, a candidate must fulfill the following criteria to appear for the exam :

● The age limit for the candidate is 21 Years to 30 Years. However, there is a provision for age relaxation for SC/ST and OBC (NCL).

● The candidate should have a graduation degree from a recognized University or any equivalent qualification recognized as such by the Central Government. If you are the Final year/Semester of your graduation course, you can also apply but you must provide proof of passing the graduation examination later. Candidates with Chartered Accountant qualification can also apply.

● Before filling the SBI PO 2020 application form don't forget to go through the eligibility section. At any stage, if it is found that a candidate doesn't meet the criteria, the application will not be acceptable.

Exam Fee

● You can pay SBI PO 2020 application fee online through Credit card, Debit card, and Internet Banking.

● There is no offline payment of the application fee of the exam

● The fees are non-refundable and non-transferable for any future exam.

● The Application Fee for SC/ST/PWD is Rs. 125 (Intimation charges only)

● For General/OBC/EWS, the fees are Rs. 750 (Inclusive of intimation charges)

How to Apply

Official website : https://www.sbi.co.in

Step 1: First click on “CAREERS Click Here” and then on “Latest Announcements”.

Step 2: You need to click on an option “Apply Online” for the Application Form.

Step 3: Read the instructions carefully to register yourself and then click on “Continue”.

Step 4: You will be asked to enter your name, mobile number, and email ID.

Step 5: Once you have provided your basic details, enter the security code, and click on “Save & Next”.

Step 6: Save your Provisional Registration Number and Password that you will get via SMS and Email.

Step 7: Now you will have to upload scanned images of your Photograph and Signature in the prescribed specifications.

Step 8: Click on “Next” and fill out your personal information, category, postal address, educational and experience details.

Step 9: Check all the entries carefully and confirm them by clicking on “Final submit”.

Step 10: Finally, pay for your SBI PO 2020 application form. Don't forget to take a print out of the form and confirmation page for future reference.

Benefits of taking the EXAM

Salary is an attraction to the aspirants of the SBI PO exam. If you qualify for the SBI PO exam, you are posted as a probationary officer with a good salary structure. The initial basic pay of SBI PO is Rs 27620 but it gradually increases to Rs 42020 after a few years. SBI PO salary also includes a number of allowances like city compensatory allowance, house rent allowance, medical insurance, traveling allowance, and many more. You will also enjoy perks like pensions, medical aid for family, leave fare concession, concessional interest rates for loans, etc. Overall, a PO gets a big exposure in terms of a bright career.

Career Scope after Exam

On qualifying the SBI PO exam, you become a part of the most prestigious bank of the country. Besides high salary, allowances, and perks, you can still increase the job standard(profile) to reach a higher position. You can appear for SBI promotion exams which are conducted annually. However, you can grab a promotion, after serving a two-year probation as a PO in the SBI bank. Eventually, you attain a bright future once you qualify for the SBI PO exam.
