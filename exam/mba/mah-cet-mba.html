About MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) Exam

MAH-CET (MBA Entrance), full form, Maharashtra Common Entrance Test is a computer-based online exam. The state government of Maharashtra conducts MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam for admission in top MBA institutes within the state. However, the DTE is responsible for the admission process and the test. This exam is a Common Entrance Exam (CET) specifically for MBA courses. The candidates writing the MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam get an opportunity to seek admission to top colleges like JBIMS, PUMBA, KJ Somaiya, etc. With the MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) students from all India category can also get seats in MBA institutes in Maharashtra. The scope of MBA in India is vast, and MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam offers students many opportunities. The exam takes place once a year during February or March. The registration process for MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam starts around the 2nd or 3rd week of January.

The Exam Pattern

  • Logical Reasoning (No of Questions 70)
    (Time Duration 37.5 Mins)
  • Intelligence and Critical Reasoning (No of Questions 25)
    (Time Duration 37.5 Mins)
  • Data Analysis and Sufficiency (No of Questions 50)
    (Time Duration 37.5 Mins)
  • Mathematical Skills (No of Questions 50)
    (Time Duration 37.5 Mins)

The test total time is: 150 Mins

Exam Dates

The state conducts MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) once a year between February or March. This year the state conducted MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam on March 14th and 15th in two morning and afternoon sessions. The state usually declares the result for MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) on 31st March 2020. But due to the countrywide lockdown because of the COVID-19 outbreak, MAH CET result was postponed to 23rd May 2020.

The Score Validity

The score obtained by the students after giving the MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam is valid only during the year in which the candidate has given the MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam.

Exam Eligibility

Nationality: The candidates who are residents of India and belong to- NRI, OCI, PIO, can apply for MAH-CET (MBA Entrance) exam.

Degree: The candidate must have a Bachelor’s Degree of 3 years in any discipline.

Appearing: The final year undergraduate students from recognised colleges and universities are eligible to appear for the MAH-CET (MBA Entrance).

Marks: The minimum marks required by candidates is an aggregate of 50%. Candidates belonging to reserved and disabled categories (who belong to Maharashtra state only) need an aggregate of 45%.

Reserved Category/ ST/SC: Candidates belonging to reserved category need to provide a Caste Validity Certificate issued by Scrutiny Committee of Social Welfare Department. Candidates, who belong to ST, should provide a Tribal Validity Certificate issued by Scrutiny Committee of Tribal Department. Reserved candidates who don’t belong to SC/ST need to provide a valid Non-creamy layer certificate.

Exam Fee

The applicants need to pay application fees of INR 1000 for general category and INR 800 for reserved category.

Cancellation Fee:

There is no refund or cancellation fee provided if the candidate cancels the MAH-SET (MBA Entrance) application. If a candidate has filed an application form and cancelled the application, they will not receive any refund. The fees paid for the application are non-refundable.

GATE Rescheduling Fee:

The same goes for GATE Rescheduling. The fees paid by the candidate are non-transferable and non-refundable as mentioned on the official MAH-SET website.

How to Apply for the MAH-SET (MBA Entrance)?

Step 1: The first step is to visit the official website of MAH CET.

Step 2: The next step requires you to click on the link “Click here for Online Registration.”

Step 3: The candidate, then, needs to read all the important instructions mentioned.

Step 4: The candidates need to register themselves after clicking on “New Registration.” Then fill in the necessary details.

Step 5: After the submission of the required details, the candidate’s application number is generated.

Step 6: After this, the candidate needs to upload certain documents. The candidate needs to make sure the scanned ../../images are clear or else the student cannot submit the form.

Step 7: The next step is to complete the application form with providing the information like personal, academics and contact details. The candidate should keep the documents with them beforehand to avoid errors.

Step 8: After the applicant has uploaded all the documents successfully, the candidate is directed to the payment gateway.

Step 9: The applicant has to pay the fees via online modes like credit/debit cards, internet banking, mobile wallets, etc. along with service charges.

Step 10: Once the payment is completed, the applicant will receive an e-receipt. The applicant should print the e-receipt as well as the application form on completion for future reference.
