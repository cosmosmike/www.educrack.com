ABOUT CAMPUS RECRUITMENT EXAMS/ CAMPUS PLACEMENTS EXAM

A plethora of job vacancies is released by the various private and public sector organizations each year. Accordingly, companies come to the college campus, conduct interviews, and hire suitable candidates to work in their company. Pursuing the campus placements programme, students get a great chance to take lucrative jobs during the course of their academic pursuits.

Hence, it is vital for the final year students to prepare adequately for these exams and make sure to have a bright future. The best way to lead in campus placement is by understanding the complete procedure and adapt wisely. These college recruitment exams are standardized aptitude tests that are meant to access quantitative ability, logical reasoning, and verbal ability. The formats of the recruitment exams may vary based on the positions you are applying for. Moreover, companies may have cut-offs in all sections, while some may have no negative marking. So enquiring beforehand about the campus exam is a good idea.

The Exam Dates

Commonly, the campus placement season begins at the pre-final/final semester. It means that the placement process may start from second last year or in the last year itself. Regardless, the actual date depends on the college, the companies arriving and the performance of the students. Last year, the recruitment exams commenced in the month of July and went on until the end of the academic year. Students should know that the placement session for any company generally has 3 –4 rounds.

THE SCORE VALIDITY

Nowadays, almost all campus recruitment exams held in India are based on a percentile system. Students obtain marks in each section separately which is then summed up to arrive at the total score. Subsequently, these scores will determine the rank for each of the candidates. For instance, the candidate with the highest marks out of 400 among all CMAT test-takers will secure All India Rank 1. Once the rank is known, the percentile calculation formula is used to determine the aggregate percentile of the candidate.

Exam Eligibility

Whether it is on-campus or off-campus placement drives, the criteria for recruitment varies from company to company. Well established companies recruit for various profiles from one college. According to the job vacancy, the criteria for each profile is also different. However, a few things that are common with all recruitment exams held over India are as follows:

● For well-paid posts, the general eligibility criteria for most companies is 60% aggregate. It implies that you should score at least 60% in your average percentage score in all semesters. However, some companies prefer a 70% aggregate. If you are highly ambitious, you should score at least 60% right from the first semester and above 70% for better opportunities.

● Besides considering your percentage, companies take into account your long-term potential, enthusiasm and passion, problem-solving skills, patience, ambition, team player capabilities, and positive attitude. While attending the placement programs, make sure to stay active and present in all rounds. Any active backlogs at the time of the placement season aren't acceptable.

Exam Fee

Corporate companies come to the campus to find talented prospects to fill their posts. Organizing a placement drive in colleges is a challenging task and after so many hustles, the company won't go empty-handed. However, to attend the placement protocol and appear for the exam, you need to fill an application form along with fees. Generally, OBC and EWS students have to pay an application fee of Rs 350 but you should note that the fees differ in different colleges.

However, SC/ST, women, PwD candidates, and ex-servicemen are exempted to pay any application fee in all universities. Before the last date, you can pay your fees via credit card/debit /net banking. If you are not shortlisted in the examination, your fees will not be refunded. The same principle is followed in the cancellation of your form. Once your application has submitted, there is no way to cancel it. Still, if you apply for the GATE exam and you want to cancel it, it is advisable to attend the exam as there is no limit for attempts.

How to Apply

For on-campus placements, your college will execute most of the recruitment formalities. However, if you couldn't discover a job opportunity in your campus, you can apply you with off-campus placements as follows:

Step 1: Check company websites for updates related to post vacancies. Once you find a vacancy that suits your resume, fill it up.

Step 2: Also, browse through the online job hosting portals. You can also choose to apply through popular and reliable online portals.

Step 3: Take a recruitment test like the AMCAT test and get a good score.

Step 4: Ask your college Principal to give a letter of recommendation in case you couldn't apply for on-campus placements for valid reasons.

Step 5: Stay connected within your network as some companies prefer recruits referred by current employees. Try to build your network and remain in touch with friends, seniors, and relatives.

Benefits of taking the EXAM

Campus placement aims at identifying talented and qualified professionals before they complete their education. Students have to give campus recruitment exams and score high for better prospects. These exams are conducted to test the candidate’s problem-solving ability and to test how efficiently a student can respond to a task. During the preparation for this exam, students have to enhance their quantitative aptitudes and brush up their skills.

Their verbal ability, analytical reasoning plus proficiency in data interpretation is also tested. Many students don't understand that these exams are conducted to brighten up their talents and lead their future on the right path. Hereby, it is the responsibility of companies training on placement to make the students equipped on all aspects of career development along with creating a good impact on them.

Career Scope after Exam

After pursuing recruitment exams, students can pursue an internship program. Nowadays, students are quite interested in internships to gain valuable work or research experience. Whether joined a part-time or full-time internship, the program helps the students to decide if the field is right for them or not. Here they will gain confidence, enhance their skills, and build a productive network with professionals in the field. Eventually, after appearing for the campus exams, they get an immense scope to explore a career path according to their choice.
