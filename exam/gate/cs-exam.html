ABOUT GATE EXAMINATION

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is an examination that primarily tests the comprehensive understanding of undergraduate subjects in engineering and science. The exam is conducted jointly by the Indian Institute of Science and seven Indian Institutes of Technologies at Roorkee, Delhi, Guwahati, Kanpur, Kharagpur, Chennai (Madras) and Mumbai (Bombay) on behalf of the National Coordination Board – GATE, Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Human Resources Development (MHRD), Government of India.


IMPORTANCE

GATE scores enable a student to gain admission into the Postgraduate Program at Indian higher education institutes, as well as jobs in various Public Sector Companies. GATE is known to be one of the most competitive exams in India. GATE scores not only hold value in India but also across various institutions across the globe.

About GATE – CS Exam

GATE – CS is a concept oriented exam. GATE exam is neither easy not hard it is moderate in such a way the best among various aspirants could be selected. Computer Science is very conceptual you don’t have to mug up something. It is a national level entrance exam held by GATE committee in eight zones across India every year. The applicants can use GATE score or rank for admission in postgraduate.

The Exam Pattern

  • General Aptitude (No of Questions 15)
    (Duration in Mins 60)
  • Engineering mathematics (No of Questions 15)
    (Duration in Mins 60)
  • Section B to H (No of Questions 35)
    (Max. Marks 60)

Total Questions are 65 and The test total time is: 3 Hours


Candidates should study some important topics for GATE – CS examination

● Programming and Data Structures

● Operating Systems

● Computer Organization and Architecture

● Algorithms

● Theory of Computation

● Database

● Computer Networking

THE SCORE VALIDITY

Score validity of GATE – CS examination is of three years. Candidates can use that score for admission in colleges for three years. After three years the scores will be invalid. Once the duration expires, candidates have to reappear for the examination in order to get new scores

Exam Eligibility

Final year student or graduate of Bachelor’s degree (or equivalent) in engineering/ architecture or Master’s degree (or equivalent) in science, computer applications can apply for GATE

Benefits of taking the EXAM

The GATE exam can be used for recruitment into various research institutes. The Council of Scientific and Industrial Research provides the award of Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and Senior Research Fellowship (SRF) to engineers who have qualified the GATE exam

Career Scope after Exam

There is a lot of scope in GATE CSE. If you crack the GATE exam you can go for further study from top universities of India like IITs, IISC Bangalore Or you can go for PSU like ISRO, DRDO, Indian oil etc.
